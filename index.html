History

Zara was founded in 1975 by Amancio Ortega and Rosalía Mera. Today, it is part of the Inditex Group, which is one of the world's largest fashion retailers. Zara operates more than 2,200 stores in 96 countries, offering its customers the latest fashion trends at affordable prices.

Zara's business model is based on fast fashion, which means it designs and produces its clothing quickly and efficiently, so that it can respond to changing trends and customer demand. The company produces over 450 million items per year, and it takes just two weeks for a new design to go from concept to store shelves.

Brand story

Zara's success can be attributed to its fast-fashion business model, which allows it to quickly respond to changing trends and customer demand. The brand's design team creates new designs and collections every few weeks, and these are then manufactured in Zara's own factories and distributed to its stores around the world. One of Zara's key strengths is its ability to stay on top of the latest fashion trends while still maintaining its unique brand identity. The brand's designers draw inspiration from a wide range of sources, including street style, art, and music, and they work closely with Zara's in-house production team to ensure that new designs can be manufactured and distributed quickly.
